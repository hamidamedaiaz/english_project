Electrolysis reaction chamber — Research Lab, Geneva · March 2024

Our proprietary nano-catalyst enables real-time water electrolysis at automotive scale — a breakthrough previously dismissed as thermodynamically impractical. The compact 4-litre reaction chamber integrates seamlessly with existing internal combustion architecture, requiring zero permanent modification to the host vehicle.

By generating hydrogen on-demand at the point of consumption, WATERFLASH eliminates all risks associated with pressurized hydrogen storage — solving the two biggest barriers to hydrogen adoption: safety and infrastructure. No tank. No station. Just water.

Our catalyst operates at ambient temperature and pressure, unlike industrial electrolysis systems. This was made possible by a nano-structured platinum-palladium alloy surface that reduces the activation energy of the splitting reaction by 74%.

Water-Powered Car — Live Demonstration · Geneva, March 2024

This is the footage that changed everything. On March 14th, 2024, in the presence of Prof. Anton Müller from ETH Zürich and two independent observers from the European Clean Energy Institute, our vehicle ran continuously for over 6 hours on nothing but tap water.

Watch closely: water is poured directly into the tank. The engine starts. The car moves. No petrol. No battery. No hydrogen canister. The only exhaust visible is water vapour — the same you see above a hot cup of tea. The efficiency meter holds steady at 95%. The output: enough propulsion to drive a family car from Paris to Lyon without stopping once.

Live Retrofit — VW Golf VII · Beta Vehicle #14 · Lyon, April 2024

Mohammed B., a 38-year-old teacher from Lyon, drove his 2018 Volkswagen Golf VII into our service center on a Tuesday morning. By 6 PM the same day, his car was running on water. No permanent modifications were made. The original petrol engine was untouched. The WATERFLASH™ Module v2.1 was mounted in the existing engine bay void using four standard bolts and a single water inlet connection.

When he drove out, his fuel gauge showed a full 10-litre water tank. His estimated cost per kilometre dropped from €0.12 to less than €0.01. He has not visited a petrol station since.

Highway Test — Lyon → Marseille, 520 km Non-Stop · May 2024

On May 8th 2024, our VP Engineering Sarah Chen set off from Lyon on the A7 motorway at 6:14 AM, driving a retrofitted Peugeot 508. The car was fuelled with 10 litres of tap water taken directly from the hotel bathroom that morning. She arrived in Marseille 3 hours and 48 minutes later — having covered 520 km at an average speed of 137 km/h — without stopping once.

The onboard data recorder shows continuous hydrogen generation throughout the entire journey, stable engine temperature, and a single exhaust byproduct: water vapour at 42°C. The petrol indicator was disconnected before departure. The car does not know it is no longer running on gasoline.

TÜV Rheinland Independent Emission Report — Ref. TR-2024-8831

We submitted our retrofitted vehicle to TÜV Rheinland in Cologne for a full well-to-wheel lifecycle emission analysis — the most comprehensive independent test available in Europe. Their conclusion, published in March 2024: absolute zero CO₂-equivalent emissions. Not "near zero." Not "offset." Absolute zero.

The exhaust analysis found no NOx, no particulates, no carbon monoxide, and no hydrocarbons. The only substance detected at the tailpipe was water vapour at ambient temperature — cleaner than the air entering the engine intake. The full report is available to accredited investors upon request.

Any Water. Anywhere. — Compatibility Tests Across 5 Countries

During our 2023–2024 field trials, we intentionally tested the limits of our filtration system. We filled the tank with Seine river water in Paris, rainwater collected on a rooftop in Marseille, mineral water from a supermarket in Zurich, tap water from 11 different cities, and filtered Mediterranean seawater off the coast of Nice.

The onboard pre-filtration module processed every single source in under 30 seconds and delivered consistent hydrogen output within ±2% variance. The implication is profound: a WATERFLASH™-equipped vehicle can be refuelled anywhere on Earth where water exists — which is everywhere.

Beta Cohort 1 — 50 Real Drivers, 12 Cities, 6 Months

In October 2023, we handed the keys of 50 retrofitted vehicles to ordinary drivers — teachers, delivery workers, nurses, retirees — across France and Switzerland. We gave them one instruction: drive normally, fill up with water, and tell us everything.

Six months later, the data speaks for itself. Average monthly fuel cost: €3.80 (the price of tap water). Average satisfaction score: 9.4 out of 10. Zero mechanical incidents. Zero range anxiety reported. Jean-Michel R. from Paris summed it up best: "I haven't been to a petrol station in four months. I fill up at my kitchen sink. I genuinely cannot explain it — but it works."
